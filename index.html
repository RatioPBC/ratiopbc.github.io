Ratio

Public Benefit Company

Helping government and healthcare organizations move data where, when, and how they need it.

Ratio is a technology consulting company that builds modern, performant, resilient software that helps government and healthcare organizations improve the health and well-being of the communities they serve.

Who We Serve

Throughout the COVID-19 pandemic, we've worked in partnership with leading NGOs and public health agencies to build and manage open source software that has improved the efficiency of public health interventions like contact tracing and case investigation. As we look beyond this public health emergency, we're committed to contributing to the technology infrastructure desperately needed by the people and organizations that deliver critical health and human services.

Government Agencies

We help public health and human services agencies integrate the data systems that underpin vital public services.

Healthcare Organizations

We help enterprise health systems and health plans speed up their innovation cycles.

Digital Health

We help digital health companies navigate the complexity of integrating with large organizations and incumbent technologies.

CENTER ME: How We Work

Work in Public

We believe trust is earned through vulnerability. That's why we hold ourselves to a standard of transparency with our partners -- when things are going well, and when we hit bumps in the road. We also advocate for publishing our work whenever possible, and have contributed several projects to the open source community. See our published work on Github.

Resilience is Paramount

Our customers deliver services that people depend on for their health and well-being. We take that responsibility to heart when making architectural decisions by building fault-tolerant software that expects the unexpected.

We're Nimble Amidst Giants

Government and healthcare organizations work on a scale that often makes it difficult to move with quickness or flexibility -- and we help them fill that gap. We're experienced working with large, incumbent data systems not designed for interoperability, and we often deliver value by finding a way to make it work (quickly) nonetheless.
